About Me

I'm a former MD-PhD student looking to pivot my career towards the tech sector as I've found such work more compatible with my personal interests and life-style. At the moment, I'm building-up my skills in data analysis and machine-learning. My skills are always growing and include Python, R, SQL, Github, Excel and, as you can see (hopefully) some HTML. Feel free to check out my projects.

I have a B.S. in biochemistry and a minor in data-science from Notre Dame, half a year of medical school coursework at the Stritch School of Medicine (Loyola), as well as four years of experience in medical research including the following in reverse chronological order:

  • A year-long post-baccalaureate at NIH performing Molecular Characterization of Human B Cell Responses to HPV-VLP under Dr. Susan Pierce
  • Two and a half years of undergraduate research on Generating a Recombinant Protein for Transgenic Insertion into Bombyx mori under Dr. Malcolm Frasier
  • A three-month internship on Attenuation of Inflammation Results in Seizure Reduction and Neuroprotection in TNFR1 Knockout and Low Inoculum TMEV under Dr. Charles Howe
  • A three-month high-school mentorship program on Investigating the Roles of Estrogen Receptors Alpha and Beta and their Role in Breast Cancer under Dr. John Hawse
